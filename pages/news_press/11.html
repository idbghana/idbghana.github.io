IDB Ghana Opens Six additional branches in Accra, Kumasi and Sefwi Asawinso

The future and prospects of every business is seen in its ability to grow and expand its services to the customers. Considered one of the effective ways of bringing banking to the doorstep of customers, branch expansion seems to be the new way of spreading risk and building confidence in the minds of customers that they can trust their investments in your capable hands.  This is what the latest entrant into the Banking industry -IDB Ghana, seems to be telling its customers and the general public.

In a bid to continue providing excellent banking services to its customers, IDB Ghana has opened six (6) additional Branches in Accra, Kumasi and Sefwi Asawinso in the western region. The opening of these branches brings to 11 the total number of branches, the bank has opened within 1 year of its operations. The Managing Director, Mr. Robert Bentil, in an interview said that the Bank is working hard to cover a lot of grounds to make its presence felt in all the major regions of the country as envisaged in the three (3) year strategic plan of the Bank. He further explained that, as a wholly owned Ghanaian Bank, IDB Ghana will be focusing on some critical sectors of the economy; key amongst them is agro processing.  “We recoginse that the Ghanaian economy is mainly agrarian, so financing the agro processing industry means we are offering substantial support to a critical sector of the economy of Ghana”

Speaking on the expansion drive of the Bank, Mr. Bentil revealed that, the Bank has envisaged in its medium term strategic plan to open 25 branches by 2015 to spread its presence in most of the country’s regional capital. This he stressed is part of the Bank’s ultimate objective of bringing IDB Ghanaing experience to the doorstep of our cherished customers and also in direct response to the numerous appeals from both existing and potential customers.

In espousing some of the achievements of the bank within this short period, Mr. Bentil emphasized the contribution to some indigenous companies to help expand their businesses and also compete favourably with their competitors.  He assured customers and the general public that, the Bank will ultimately expand its operations to other Regions of the Country. He urged the banking public to embrace the unique services of the Bank, with the pledge that the Bank, true to its tagline, will continue to deliver Real Banking with Real Difference.
